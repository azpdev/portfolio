Education

Salt Lake Community College

Generals and Core Computer Science Classes

My opinion on the education system as a whole (in the US at least) is not very high. I didn't do particularly well in high school. Failed/incomplete classes my sophomore year, which made my GPA quite low, under 3.0. The rest of my time, I coasted. I didn't even finish a full year of math. I got just enough math credits to graduate. I got my GPA back up to just above 3.5. It was easy to get A's and not hard not to try much to get them. Community is a good route for everyone as it is cheaper and you have the ability to try more things if you are undecided. I was not undecided then.

My view on college is not exactly much better. Most of the generals were useless and boring and best case they were a repeat of classes in high school. I had been in a few AP classes in high school and they were drastically harder than the college equivalents or similar courses. So for the most part only thing, the college classes were going to get me was credentialism. However, I did really enjoy my philosophy courses. The professor was phenomenal, and I got a newfound interest. I also picked up Jiu Jitsu after a discussion board mentioned trying out kick boxing in my online gym class.

I did finally finish a math class. I took the classes that were prerequisites for Calculus. The college wanted me to go back to Algebra… I made it five minutes in the class before dropping it and getting into the harder class. I did half of AP Calculus in high school, but it started to get quite difficult about halfway through when not finishing previous math classes started to cause issues. I also took calculus as well.

The core computer science classes were not terrible. I took Intro to Computer Programming in High School, and that is what sparked my interest in programming. I skipped the first class they wanted me to because it was too basic. It was more for people who were interested in programming but had never done it before. Most of the classes were in Java. Object Oriented, Web Programming, Discrete Mathematics, Algorithms, Software Development, and Computer Architecture. Learned a decent amount in these courses. My overall GPA was high 3.9. Software development needs a trade schools and boot camps don't quite do what they need to so college is the best bet right now.

Utah Valley University

Associates in General Studies

Certification of Completion: Programmer

After getting all the classes I could from SLCC, I transferred to UVU to finish my degree. I had enough credits for an associate but not the correct courses.

At UVU I took all Computer courses. Networking, more discrete math, OS Theory, more algorithms, C++, and more web programming. I had some good experiences in these courses.

I did finally get my first software job at this point. Enviably, I had gotten what I wanted from school. I learned a lot, and I can continue learning without the structure. Also, the experience has been way more valuable, and I can always go back. I was able to apply for both my associate and certificate of completion programmer after my first semester.

I did fail one class when my workload and school load combined was a lot. My least liked class, Numerical software development, took a hit. It also really needed another prerequisite of Linear Algebra. It required you really to learn Numerical Software Development concepts and Linear Algebra at the same time. I wasn't about to let all my classes suffer, so giving up just one was the best option.
